# Fitness & Health App Intelligence Repository

> Comprehensive competitive intelligence on 50+ fitness, nutrition, recovery, and mindfulness apps across iOS and Android. 20,000+ lines of research.

## Purpose

This repository serves as a centralized competitive intelligence database for evaluating the fitness and health app landscape. It provides detailed profiles on each major app including UX/UI flows, feature inventories, business metrics, user demographics, and go-to-market strategies.

## Per-App Profile Template

Each app profile includes 11 standardized sections:

1. **Overview** — What it is, platforms, founding date, parent company
2. **User Demographics** — Age, gender, fitness level, geography
3. **Core Features** — Complete feature inventory
4. **UX/UI Flow** — Screen-by-screen click-through paths (onboarding → core loop → monetization)
5. **Design Language** — Visual style, color palette, typography, interaction patterns
6. **Monetization** — Pricing tiers, conversion funnels, paywall placement
7. **Business Metrics** — Downloads, MAU, revenue, ratings
8. **Go-to-Market** — Launch strategy, partnerships, influencer deals
9. **Content Strategy** — Video, social media, blog, email marketing
10. **Strengths & Weaknesses** — What works, what doesn't
11. **Key Takeaways** — Lessons and competitive positioning

## Key Findings

### Top Whitespace Opportunities
1. **No app integrates all 4 pillars** (training + nutrition + recovery + mindfulness)
2. **AI-adaptive cross-modal periodization** doesn't exist yet
3. **GLP-1 medication tracking** is the biggest emerging integration opportunity
4. **Adults 50+** are massively underserved
5. **Manual logging fatigue** remains the #1 UX problem across all nutrition apps

### Revenue Insights
- Fitness app median trial-to-paid conversion: **39.9%** (top 10%: 68.3%)
- Revenue per install: **$0.63** (highest of any app category)
- The $60-80/year price point is where market leaders cluster
- Hardware+software models command highest LTV ($500-2,000)

### GTM Playbooks That Work
- **Quiz funnels** (Noom, BetterMe): High conversion but trust risk
- **Podcast-driven** (TrainerRoad, WHOOP): Capital-efficient, builds authority
- **Zero-marketing organic** (Hevy, Strava): Social loops + generous free tier
- **Celebrity/influencer-led** (SWEAT, Centr): Fast launch but personality risk
