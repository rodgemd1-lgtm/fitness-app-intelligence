## 1. Overview

### What It Is

Therabody is a wellness technology company best known for the Theragun line of percussive therapy massage guns. The Therabody app serves as the digital companion and control hub for its expanding hardware ecosystem, which includes massage guns (Theragun), compression boots (RecoveryAir/JetBoots), eye/temple massager (SmartGoggles), vibrating rollers (WaveRoller, WaveDuo, WaveSolo), LED therapy wraps (ThermBack), and facial devices (TheraFace PRO). The app connects via Bluetooth to all compatible devices and provides guided recovery routines, AI-powered personalized recovery plans, body mapping for soreness targeting, breathwork protocols, and wellness content spanning sleep, stress, and pain management.

### Founding & History

| Milestone | Date | Detail |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Origin Story | 2007 | Dr. Jason Wersland, a chiropractor, suffered a debilitating motorcycle accident and began experimenting with percussive therapy for pain relief |
| First Prototype | 2009 | Wersland bought 250 Kawasaki jigsaws, disassembled them, retrofitted them, and sold them as early percussive devices |
| Iterations | 2008-2015 | Five different versions developed over 7 years |
| Patent Filed | 2015 | Finalized a version that could be patented |
| Theragun G1 Launch | June 2016 | First consumer product officially launched |
| Rebrand to Therabody | 2020 | Company rebranded from Theragun to Therabody to reflect broader wellness technology portfolio |
| $165M Growth Equity | September 2022 | Series A led by North Castle Partners; launched 8 new products simultaneously |
| $10M Research Pledge | 2023 | Committed $10M to scientific research partnerships and established Scientific Advisory Board |
| AI Coach Launch | 2024 | Introduced "Coach by Therabody" AI-powered personalized recovery planning |

### Parent Company

Therabody, Inc. is a private, venture-backed company headquartered in Los Angeles, California. Founded by Dr. Jason Wersland (Founder & Chief Wellness Officer). The company employs 500-1,000 people.

## 4. UX/UI Flow

### Onboarding Flow

1. **Account Creation** -- Email/password registration; collect basic profile info
2. **Device Pairing** -- Bluetooth scan to connect compatible Therabody hardware
3. **Goal Setting** -- Select recovery goals (performance, pain relief, sleep, stress)
4. **Activity Integration** -- Connect Garmin, Strava, Google Fit for activity data
5. **Body Scan** -- Tap on anatomical illustration to indicate sore/problem areas
6. **First Guided Routine** -- AI Coach recommends a personalized routine based on inputs
7. **Post-Routine Summary** -- Session data tracked, next recommendations provided

### Core Usage Loop

```
Open App --> Body Scan (tap sore areas) --> AI Coach Recommends Routine -->
Pair Device (auto-connect) --> Follow Guided Routine (device auto-adjusts) -->
Post-Session Tracking --> Next Day Updated Plan
```

### Key Screens

| Screen | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Home / Dashboard** | AI Coach daily recovery plan, activity sync status, recommended routines |
| **Body Map** | Interactive anatomical illustration for identifying problem areas |
| **Routines Library** | Browse by body area, goal, device type, duration |
| **Device Control** | Real-time speed, pattern, and intensity controls for connected hardware |
| **Activity Feed** | Synced workouts from wearables and fitness apps |
| **Profile / History** | Completed sessions, recovery trends, therapy data |

### Navigation Pattern

- Device-centric flow: the app experience is anchored around which Therabody products you own
- Users select products they own and programs filter based on their device ecosystem
- Bottom tab navigation on mobile

## 6. Monetization

### Pricing Model

| Element | Price | Detail |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **App Download** | Free | App is free to download and use |
| **Basic Routines** | Free | Core recovery routines available to all users |
| **Premium Content** | Varies | Some advanced content and features tied to device ownership tier |
| **Hardware (Theragun)** | $199-$599 | Relief ($199), Prime ($299), Elite ($399), PRO Plus ($599) |
| **Hardware (Compression)** | $599-$999+ | RecoveryAir JetBoots and RecoveryAir PRO |
| **Hardware (Accessories)** | $149-$299 | SmartGoggles ($199), TheraFace PRO ($299), WaveRoller ($149) |

### Revenue Model

- **Hardware-first monetization**: The app is primarily a vehicle to drive hardware sales and enhance device value
- **Razor-and-blade inverted**: Hardware is the high-margin product (not the subscription); the free app justifies the hardware premium
- **Upsell ecosystem**: Users who buy one device are exposed to the full product ecosystem through the app, driving multi-device adoption
- **Professional channel**: Physical therapists and chiropractors recommend Therabody devices to patients, creating a B2B2C referral loop

### What's Behind the Paywall

- Recent app update controversy: the "my routines" feature was moved behind device-tier restrictions, requiring purchase of higher-end devices to access previously available functionality
- Premium content layers are emerging but the core app remains free to support hardware sales

## 11. Key Takeaways for Fitness App Startups

### What to Learn From Therabody

1. **Hardware creates lock-in that software alone cannot.** Therabody's app is free because the hardware generates the revenue and the switching costs. For app-only startups, this means finding other forms of lock-in (data history, personalization, community) since you cannot rely on physical device dependency.

2. **Science and clinical credibility justify premium pricing.** Therabody's $10M research pledge and chiropractor founder are not marketing gimmicks -- they create a defensible moat against cheaper alternatives. Startups should invest in clinical validation, expert partnerships, and evidence-based content early.

3. **The body mapping interaction model is powerful.** Letting users tap where they hurt and receiving personalized recommendations creates an intuitive, empathetic UX. Any recovery or fitness app should consider body-based input as a primary interaction pattern.

4. **Celebrity investors are a GTM channel, not just a funding source.** Therabody's 100+ celebrity investors serve as organic brand ambassadors. Startups should consider strategic angel investors who will actively promote the product to their audiences.

5. **Professional recommendation channels are high-trust funnels.** Physical therapists recommending Therabody to patients creates a referral loop that paid advertising cannot replicate. Startups should explore partnerships with healthcare professionals, trainers, and coaches.

### What to Avoid

1. **Do not gate previously free features behind hardware tiers.** Therabody's decision to restrict "my routines" to higher-end device owners created user backlash. Never take away value that users have already experienced.

2. **Do not build an app with zero standalone value.** If your app requires a $300+ hardware purchase to be useful, you limit your addressable market dramatically. Ensure the app delivers meaningful value even without proprietary hardware.

3. **Do not neglect social and community features in recovery.** Recovery is often a solitary experience, but accountability, progress sharing, and peer encouragement can dramatically improve adherence. Therabody misses this entirely.

### Strategic Opportunities Therabody Leaves Open

| Opportunity | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Software-only recovery platform** | Therabody requires $199-$599 hardware; an app-only recovery platform with guided routines, body mapping, and AI personalization at $10-15/month captures the hardware-resistant market |
| **Community-driven recovery** | Therabody has zero social features; a recovery app with accountability groups, progress sharing, and peer support fills this gap |
| **Affordable body mapping** | The tap-where-it-hurts UX is brilliant but locked behind Therabody's ecosystem; this interaction pattern can exist in any recovery app |
| **Cross-device agnostic recovery** | Therabody only controls Therabody devices; a platform that integrates with any massage gun, foam roller, or recovery tool via generic Bluetooth or manual logging has broader appeal |
