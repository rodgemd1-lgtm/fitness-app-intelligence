## 1. Overview

### What It Is

Pliability is a guided mobility, stretching, and recovery app that delivers daily follow-along video routines designed to improve flexibility, reduce pain, accelerate recovery, and enhance athletic performance. Originally built for the CrossFit community as ROMWOD (Range of Motion Workout of the Day), the app rebranded to Pliability in September 2022 to broaden its appeal beyond CrossFit to runners, golfers, HYROX competitors, weightlifters, cyclists, yogis, and general fitness enthusiasts. The app offers 1,700+ guided routines with no equipment required, organized by sport, body part, goal, and concern.

### Founding & History

| Milestone | Date | Detail |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Founded | 2015 | By Scott N. Perkins and Jeremiah Head as ROMWOD (Range of Motion Workout of the Day) |
| CrossFit Adoption | 2015-2022 | Became a staple in CrossFit boxes worldwide; daily mobility WOD format resonated with CrossFit culture |
| Rebrand to Pliability | September 2022 | Shifted from CrossFit-exclusive to broad athletic audience; new brand identity and expanded content |
| WHOOP Integration | September 2022 | Launched data-driven recovery recommendations using WHOOP biometric data |
| HYROX Official Partner | May 2025 | Became the first-ever Official Stretching Partner of HYROX |
| HYROX Hub Launch | July 2025 | Dedicated in-app hub with specialized HYROX mobility programs |

### Parent Company

Pliability (formerly ROMWOD, LLC) is an independent, bootstrapped company headquartered in Wilmington, Delaware. No external funding raised as of 2025. Approximately 11 employees across 2 continents.

## 4. UX/UI Flow

### Onboarding Flow

1. **Account Creation** -- Email/password or social login
2. **Goal Selection** -- Choose primary objectives (flexibility, pain relief, sport performance, recovery)
3. **Sport/Activity Selection** -- Identify primary sports and activities for personalized recommendations
4. **Body Concern Areas** -- Indicate problem areas or focus zones
5. **WHOOP Connection (Optional)** -- Link WHOOP account for biometric-driven recommendations
6. **First Daily Routine** -- Immediately presented with today's recommended routine
7. **Post-Routine Feedback** -- Rate routine and log how you feel

### Core Usage Loop

```
Open App --> View Daily Routine or Browse Library --> Select Routine -->
Follow Along Video --> Complete Session --> Track Progress -->
Receive Next Recommendation
```

### Key Screens

| Screen | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Home / Today** | Daily recommended routine, personalized suggestions, WHOOP data integration |
| **Browse Library** | Filter by sport, body part, goal, duration; 1,700+ routines organized intuitively |
| **HYROX Hub** | Dedicated HYROX-specific mobility programs and event preparation |
| **Programs** | Multi-week structured programs for specific goals |
| **Progress** | Streak tracking, completed routines, flexibility assessment history |
| **Profile** | Account settings, WHOOP connection, subscription management |

### Navigation Pattern

- Clean, organized structure broken down by sport, body part, and concern
- Bottom tab navigation on mobile
- Search supports exercise-specific queries (find routines that improve your deadlift)
- Filters reduce browsing friction: sport, body part, goal, duration

## 5. Design Language

### Visual Identity

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Primary Theme** | Clean, modern, fitness-forward design |
| **Color Palette** | Brand uses warm tones with clean white/neutral backgrounds |
| **Typography** | Clean sans-serif; lowercase brand treatment ("pliability") for approachable feel |
| **Photography** | Professional athlete imagery in studio and gym environments |
| **Video Production** | High-quality studio-shot follow-along videos with consistent visual style |

### Design Principles

- **Clarity over complexity**: Routines show duration and muscles worked upfront, reducing decision fatigue
- **Sport-first organization**: Content is organized around the user's sport/activity, not generic categories
- **Approachable expertise**: Lowercase branding and clean design make professional mobility work feel accessible
- **Cross-platform consistency**: Consistent experience across iOS, Android, and web

### UX Precision

- Design and development handled in partnership with Utility Agency
- Focus on seamless personal recommendation engine that gets users into routines quickly
- Intuitive video tools designed for follow-along use (minimal tap interaction during routines)

## 6. Monetization

### Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Detail |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Free Trial** | $0 for 7 days | Full access to all features during trial |
| **Monthly Subscription** | $19.95/month | Full access to all routines, programs, and features |
| **Annual Subscription** | $14.99/month ($179.88/year) | 25% discount on annual commitment |

### Revenue Model

- **Pure subscription model**: No hardware, no in-app purchases, no ads -- 100% subscription revenue
- **No free tier**: After the 7-day trial, a subscription is required for any content access
- **Partnership revenue**: HYROX partnership may include revenue sharing or sponsorship fees (terms not disclosed)
- **B2B potential**: Gym and box partnerships for bulk subscriptions (aligned with CrossFit box distribution model)

### What's Behind the Paywall

- Everything. After the 7-day free trial, all content requires an active subscription
- No free routines, no limited tier, no freemium model
- This is a high-conviction monetization strategy that depends on content quality justifying the price

## 11. Key Takeaways for Fitness App Startups

### What to Learn From Pliability

1. **Community-first GTM is the most capital-efficient launch strategy.** ROMWOD built an entire business by embedding in CrossFit boxes. For a startup, find your "CrossFit" -- a passionate community with shared rituals -- and build content that fits their existing daily routine.

2. **Rebranding to expand TAM can work if the content backs it up.** Pliability successfully moved from CrossFit-only to multi-sport by adding golf, running, HYROX, and general wellness content. The lesson: do not rebrand as aspirational positioning alone; ship the content that justifies the broader identity.

3. **Partnership-led GTM can substitute for paid marketing budget.** The HYROX Official Stretching Partner deal gives Pliability exposure at every event globally with ~11 employees and zero funding. Strategic partnerships can provide distribution that would cost millions in advertising.

4. **Wearable integration creates personalization without building hardware.** The WHOOP integration lets Pliability deliver biometric-driven recovery recommendations using someone else's sensor data. Startups should integrate with existing wearables rather than building proprietary hardware.

5. **Bootstrapped is viable in fitness SaaS.** Pliability proves that a focused team can build a high-quality product, attract elite athletes, and secure major partnerships without venture capital.

### What to Avoid

1. **Do not launch with a hard paywall and no free tier.** Pliability's no-free-content approach limits viral growth and community building. Even a small free library would expand the top of funnel and allow word-of-mouth to work.

2. **Do not underinvest in brand awareness outside your origin community.** Pliability is excellent but relatively unknown outside CrossFit/HYROX circles. A broader content marketing and social media strategy would accelerate growth.

3. **Do not price at $20/month without a clear value differentiator for casual users.** Free YouTube stretching content and cheaper alternatives make the $20 price point a hard sell for non-committed users. Consider a lower entry tier.

### Strategic Opportunities Pliability Leaves Open

| Opportunity | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Freemium mobility app** | Pliability has no free tier; a generous free library of basic stretching routines would capture the top-of-funnel audience Pliability misses |
| **AI-first personalization** | Pliability uses AI for flexibility scanning but the core experience is still a video library; a truly AI-driven daily mobility plan (not just recommendations) is still unclaimed |
| **Hardware + software recovery** | Pliability is software-only; integrating with foam rollers, massage guns, or other recovery tools creates a more comprehensive experience |
| **Workplace mobility** | Pliability focuses on athletes; a desk-worker-focused mobility product (5-minute breaks, posture correction, wrist/neck relief) targets a much larger TAM |
