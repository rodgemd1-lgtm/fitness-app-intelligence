# MyPlate (by Livestrong) - Competitive Intelligence Profile

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-03
**Category:** Nutrition Tracking / Calorie Counting
**Direct Competitor Relevance:** MODERATE -- media-backed calorie tracker leveraging Livestrong brand authority and USDA food database

---

## 1. Overview

**Tagline:** "Tracking Calories Works"

- **Founded:** ~2011 (as part of Livestrong.com ecosystem, originally tied to the Lance Armstrong Foundation rebrand)
- **Headquarters:** Santa Monica, California, USA
- **Parent Company:** Leaf Group Ltd (now part of Graham Holdings Company)
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Apple Watch, Web (via livestrong.com/myplate)
- **Team Size:** Part of broader Livestrong/Leaf Group team (~696 employees across the parent org)
- **Funding:** Backed by Livestrong.com media revenue -- not independently funded
- **User Base:** 4M+ members; ~350K monthly active users (iOS); ~433K downloads/month (estimated)
- **Revenue:** Subscription + advertising revenue; parent company Livestrong.com reaches 26M monthly unique visitors

**Origin Story:** MyPlate launched as Livestrong.com's flagship tool, leveraging the media brand's massive health-content audience to funnel readers into an actionable calorie-tracking app. The name "MyPlate" aligns with the USDA's MyPlate dietary guidelines, reinforcing government-backed nutritional credibility. The app won the 22nd Annual Webby Award's People's Voice in the Best Visual Design-Function category, validating its design approach.

---

## 2. User Demographics

**Primary Segments:**
- **Mainstream calorie counters** -- users seeking a straightforward, no-frills tracker for weight loss or maintenance
- **Livestrong.com readers** -- existing audience of the health/fitness content site converted into app users
- **Beginners and casual trackers** -- people new to nutrition tracking who want simplicity over data depth
- **Women 25-54** -- primary demographic for health content sites; core Livestrong.com readership
- **Hispanic/bilingual health seekers** -- Livestrong runs targeted bilingual digital campaigns with culturally relevant influencers
- **Community-oriented users** -- people motivated by social support and message boards

**Psychographic Profile:** Mainstream health-conscious consumers who want a simple, trusted tool. Not data nerds or biohackers -- these are people who read Livestrong articles about "10 foods to eat for weight loss" and want a matching tracker. Values simplicity and brand trust over feature depth.

**Age Range:** 25-55, skewing toward the general Livestrong.com readership demographic.

## 3. Core Features

### 3.1 Calorie Tracking
- Log meals across Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Snacks
- Daily calorie goal calculated from profile data (age, weight, height, activity level, goal)
- Running calorie balance showing remaining calories for the day
- Macro breakdown (protein, carbs, fat) with visual progress indicators

### 3.2 Food Database
- **2M+ food items** sourced from USDA and brand-verified entries
- Barcode scanner for packaged foods
- Search by food name with portion size selection
- "My Meals" feature for saving and quickly re-logging frequent combinations

### 3.3 Water Tracking
- Daily water intake goal with tap-to-log functionality
- Visual progress indicator
- Reminders to stay hydrated throughout the day

### 3.4 Exercise Tracking
- Extensive exercise database for logging workouts
- Step tracking integration with Apple Health and Google Fit
- Auto-sync with Google Fit for walking, running, and biking activity
- Calories burned factored into daily energy balance

### 3.5 Meal Plans (Gold)
- Curated meal plans with recipes
- Clean Eating Guide with 21 recipes included in Gold membership
- Structured weekly plans for weight loss goals

### 3.6 Community
- Motivational message boards with 24/7 community support
- Private members board (Gold exclusive)
- Facebook community integration with recipe sharing and challenges

### 3.7 Progress Tracking
- Weight tracking with visual graphs
- Calories and macronutrient trend charts
- Apple Health integration for centralized health data

## 4. UX/UI Flow

### 4.1 Onboarding
- Profile setup: age, sex, height, current weight, goal weight
- Activity level selection
- Calorie goal automatically calculated
- Quick and frictionless -- designed to get users logging within 2-3 minutes

### 4.2 Daily Logging (Diary View)
- Meal-based chronological diary (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks)
- Add food via search, barcode scan, recent foods, or saved meals
- Remaining calorie budget prominently displayed
- Water intake tracker integrated into the daily view

### 4.3 Dashboard
- At-a-glance calorie summary for the day
- Macro breakdown with progress bars
- Exercise calories earned
- Water intake progress
- Weight trend chart

### 4.4 Community Tab
- Message boards organized by topic
- Recent community posts and discussions
- Recipe sharing and challenge participation

### 4.5 Apple Watch Integration
- Quick food logging from the wrist
- Calorie and macro summary glanceable on watch face
- Activity sync between Watch and app

## 5. Design Language

- **Color Palette:** Clean whites with green and orange accents -- reflects health and energy without clinical sterility
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif, readable and approachable
- **Data Density:** LOW to MODERATE -- prioritizes simplicity over information overload
- **Visualization Style:** Circular progress rings for daily goals, horizontal bars for macros, simple line charts for weight trends
- **Overall Aesthetic:** Clean, mainstream consumer health app. Feels accessible and non-intimidating. Designed for the general public, not power users. More "health magazine" than "nutrition lab."
- **Webby Award Winner:** Recognized for Best Visual Design-Function, validating the clean functional approach
- **Brand Integration:** Livestrong branding throughout creates trust through association with established health media

  - Private members-only community board
  - Priority customer support

### Advertising Revenue
- Free tier is ad-supported

## 8. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Primary Growth Channels
1. **Content-to-app funnel:** Livestrong.com's 26M monthly visitors represent a massive organic acquisition channel. Health articles link directly to MyPlate for actionable tracking.
2. **SEO-driven content marketing:** Livestrong.com dominates search results for queries like "calories in [food]," "how many calories to lose weight," and "best foods for weight loss" -- each a natural on-ramp to the app.
3. **Community challenges:** Facebook community campaigns where thousands of Livestrong members participate in shared challenges, share recipes, and promote healthy living goals.
4. **Bilingual/multicultural outreach:** Targeted campaigns reaching Hispanic health seekers through bilingual digital ads, earned media, and culturally relevant influencers.
5. **Brand partnerships:** Collaborations with health-adjacent brands (e.g., Hass Avocado Board partnership) to cross-promote healthy eating content and app usage.
6. **App Store Optimization:** Leveraging Livestrong brand recognition for organic app store discovery.

### Content Strategy
- **Article-app integration:** Health articles on Livestrong.com include CTAs to download MyPlate and start tracking
- **Recipe content:** Meal plans and recipe content serves dual purpose as editorial content and in-app premium feature
- **Data-driven content:** Livestrong publishes reports based on MyPlate user data (e.g., "America's Most Popular Foods") generating PR coverage and brand awareness

### Notable Absence
- Limited influencer marketing beyond parent brand
- No visible podcast sponsorship strategy
- Minimal presence in fitness creator ecosystem compared to competitors

## 9. Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths
- **Media brand moat:** Livestrong.com's 26M monthly visitors provide an organic acquisition channel that pure-play apps cannot replicate. The brand trust transfers to the app.
- **USDA-backed database:** 2M+ food items with USDA sourcing gives the database institutional credibility.
- **Simplicity as a feature:** The app does not overwhelm users with data. It is approachable for beginners who find Cronometer or MacroFactor intimidating.
- **Price-competitive Gold tier:** At $44.99/year, Gold undercuts most premium nutrition apps while offering a solid feature set.
- **Community engagement:** Active message boards and Facebook groups create social accountability that improves retention.
- **Webby Award-winning design:** Validated visual design quality.
- **Content flywheel:** Health articles drive app downloads; app data fuels new content.

### Weaknesses
- **Database quality concerns:** Users report unverified foods lacking quantity information, and the search engine is described as weak. The curated quality of Cronometer's database is absent here.
- **Android app unpublished:** Losing the Google Play presence (July 2023) eliminates roughly half the mobile market. This is a major red flag for the app's long-term viability.
- **Gold membership value questioned:** User reviews indicate Gold does not meaningfully improve the experience -- poor conversion/retention signal.
- **Technical reliability:** Reports of server crashes and features (e.g., "My Meals") not working properly damage trust.
- **No AI or smart features:** No photo logging, no adaptive recommendations, no algorithmic coaching -- increasingly table-stakes features in 2025-2026.
- **Measurement precision:** No decimal options for food quantities; does not remember portion sizes from previous days -- friction for consistent trackers.
- **Brand baggage:** Despite the rebrand, Livestrong still carries residual associations with the Lance Armstrong controversy, which may limit brand trust for some users.
- **Declining investment signals:** Android depublishing, limited feature innovation, and aging interface suggest the app may not be a strategic priority for the parent company.

## 10. Content Strategy

- **Editorial powerhouse:** Livestrong.com publishes hundreds of health, nutrition, and fitness articles monthly, many linking to MyPlate as a companion tool
- **SEO dominance:** High-authority domain ranks for thousands of nutrition-related keywords, creating a persistent top-of-funnel acquisition engine
- **Recipe content:** Clean eating guides and meal plans serve dual roles as editorial content and premium app features
- **Data journalism:** MyPlate user data powers reports on food trends (e.g., "America's Most Popular Foods") that generate press coverage
- **Community UGC:** Facebook community generates user-created recipes, progress stories, and challenge content
- **Social media:** Active presence across Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest leveraging health content
- **Limited creator economy presence:** Does not actively participate in fitness influencer or YouTube creator ecosystems

## 11. Key Takeaways for TransformFit

1. **Media-to-app funnels are powerful.** Livestrong proves that owning the content layer (articles, SEO, editorial) creates a free, perpetual user acquisition channel. TransformFit should consider a content strategy that drives organic discovery and funnels readers into the app.

2. **Simplicity has a ceiling.** MyPlate's approachable design attracts beginners but lacks the depth to retain serious users. TransformFit needs progressive complexity -- simple for beginners, powerful for advanced users -- rather than choosing one extreme.

3. **Database quality is non-negotiable.** User complaints about unverified foods and weak search are dealbreakers for retention. Investing in data accuracy (even at smaller scale) is more valuable than having millions of unreliable entries.

4. **Platform abandonment is a death signal.** Unpublishing the Android app suggests declining investment. TransformFit must commit to full cross-platform support or risk losing half its addressable market.

5. **Premium must deliver clear value.** MyPlate Gold's inability to justify its cost is a conversion and retention failure. Every premium feature should have an obvious "I cannot live without this" hook.

6. **Community is a retention lever.** MyPlate's message boards and Facebook groups create social accountability. Even basic community features (forums, challenges, streaks) can meaningfully reduce churn.

7. **Brand trust transfers, but so does brand baggage.** Leveraging an established brand accelerates growth but also imports any negative associations. Build brand equity carefully.
