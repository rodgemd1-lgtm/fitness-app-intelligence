## 3. Blog/SEO

### MyFitnessPal: The Gold Standard

**Traffic Numbers:**
- 24M+ monthly website visitors (total)
- Blog ("Hello Healthy") surged to 9.5M monthly readers from near-zero
- Blog growth drove a 22% increase in weekly app engagement

**Content Strategy:**
- Daily tips and expert-backed fitness advice
- Focus areas: healthy eating, calorie counting, meal prep, exercise guidance
- SEO-optimized articles targeting high-volume search queries
- Content calendar aligned with seasonal trends (New Year, summer, back-to-school)
- Regular publishing cadence keeps content fresh and algorithms rewarding the domain

**SEO Approach:**
- Keyword targeting: "calories in [food]", "how many calories in [food]", "macro calculator"
- Food database pages rank for millions of food-related queries
- Internal linking from blog to app features (calorie counter, barcode scanner)
- 2025: Partnership with Walmart for barcode-scanning feature created new content opportunities
- High domain authority from 65M+ registered users creating inbound links

**Key Insight:** MyFitnessPal's blog is not a marketing afterthought — it is a critical acquisition channel. Users searching for "calories in a banana" find MyFitnessPal, read the content, and discover the app. This is the single most effective SEO-to-app pipeline in fitness.

### Noom: Psychology-Driven Content

**Content Strategy:**
- Content focuses on psychology of eating and behavior change (not just calories)
- Blog topics: "Why do I eat when I'm not hungry?", "How to build lasting habits"
- Partnership with The Independent (UK): branded content targeting Noom's ideal demographic
- Health-focused SEO content improves search rankings across weight loss, behavior change, and wellness queries
- Content marketing spend increased 20% in 2024

**SEO Approach:**
- Targets long-tail queries related to weight loss psychology
- Content positions Noom as a "health program" rather than a "diet app"
- Affiliate partnerships with health bloggers and publishers paying up to $15/signup
- Authoritative health content competes with WebMD, Healthline, and Mayo Clinic for health queries

### Fitbod: Technical SEO for a Niche Audience

**Content Strategy:**
- Exercise-focused blog targeting specific workout queries
- Content: "Best exercises for [muscle group]", "How to progressive overload", "[Exercise] form guide"
- Technical accuracy positions Fitbod as an authority for evidence-based training
- Blog articles serve as standalone value AND funnel to the app's AI-generated workout feature

**SEO Approach:**
- Long-tail exercise queries (lower competition, high intent)
- Exercise database pages rank for specific movement searches
- Image optimization for exercise illustration searches
- Internal linking from exercise guides to "try it in Fitbod" CTAs

## 4. Email Marketing

### Industry Benchmarks

| Metric | Fitness App Average | Top Performers | All-Industry Average |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Open Rate (automated) | 42.1% | 50-60% | 35% |
| Open Rate (manual/campaign) | 25.1% | 30-40% | 22% |

| Unsubscribe Rate | 0.3-0.5% | <0.2% | 0.3% |
| Revenue per Email | $0.10-0.30 | $0.50-1.00 | $0.08 |

**Key Finding:** Automated, behavior-triggered emails outperform manually sent campaigns by a 1.7x margin on open rates. This makes email automation the single highest-ROI content marketing channel for fitness apps.

### Email Campaign Types

#### Onboarding Sequences

**Purpose:** Convert new users to active users within the first 7 days.

| Email | Timing | Content | Avg. Open Rate |
|-------|--------|---------|---------------|
| Welcome | Immediately | App tour, first workout suggestion | 50-65% |
| Quick Win | Day 1 | "Complete your first 10-min workout" | 40-50% |
| Social Proof | Day 3 | User testimonials, transformation stories | 35-45% |
| Feature Discovery | Day 5 | Highlight underused feature (e.g., meal logging) | 30-40% |
| Commitment | Day 7 | "Set your weekly goal" + streak introduction | 25-35% |

#### Re-Engagement Campaigns

**Purpose:** Bring back users who have been inactive for 7-30+ days.

| Trigger | Email Subject Strategy | Content | Recovery Rate |
|---------|----------------------|---------|---------------|
| 7 days inactive | "We saved your progress" | Reminder of streak/data, easy re-entry workout | 15-25% |
| 14 days inactive | "Your [goal] is waiting" | Personalized plan reminder, what they're missing | 10-18% |
| 20 days inactive | At-risk identification trigger | Automated check-in, survey, offer | 8-15% |
| 30 days inactive | "A lot has changed since you left" | New features, content, limited-time offer | 5-12% |
| 60+ days inactive | "Start fresh" | New program suggestion, seasonal promotion | 3-8% |

#### Abandoned Subscription Recovery

**Purpose:** Convert users who viewed the paywall but didn't subscribe.

**Trigger:** User explores app, reaches paywall, leaves without subscribing.

**Sequence:**
| Email | Timing | Content | Conversion Rate |
|-------|--------|---------|----------------|
| Reminder | 24 hours | "Your personalized plan is ready" | 8-12% |
| Social Proof | 48 hours | "Join X users who achieved [goal]" | 5-8% |
| Limited Offer | 72 hours | Discount or extended trial | 10-15% |
| Last Chance | 7 days | Urgency + final offer | 3-5% |

#### Progress and Milestone Emails

**Purpose:** Celebrate achievements and reinforce the habit loop.

| Milestone | Email Content | Impact on Retention |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| First workout completed | Congratulations + "what's next" | 20% higher Day-7 retention |
| 7-day streak | Badge + social sharing prompt | 15% higher Day-30 retention |
| 30-day streak | Summary report + transformation prompt | 25% higher Day-60 retention |
| Weight/fitness goal progress | Data visualization + encouragement | 30% higher LTV |
| Subscription anniversary | Year-in-review + renewal incentive | 10-15% higher renewal |

### Push Notifications vs. Email

| Channel | Avg. CTR | Best Use Case | Timing |
|---------|---------|--------------|--------|
| Push Notification | 8% avg. (12% Android, 4% iOS) | Time-sensitive (workout reminders, live class starting) | Real-time |
| Email | 2.4% CTR | Detailed content (progress reports, new features, re-engagement) | Morning or evening |
| SMS | 10-15% CTR | Urgent re-engagement, flash sales | Sparingly (opt-in) |
| In-App Message | 15-25% CTR | Feature education, upsells | During active sessions |

### Email Marketing Best Practices for Fitness Apps

1. **Segment by behavior, not demographics** — A user who logs 5x/week needs different content than a user who logged once
2. **Automate everything** — Behavior-triggered > manual campaigns (42.1% vs. 25.1% open rates)
3. **Personalize with data** — Include workout counts, progress metrics, and specific next-step recommendations
4. **Optimize send time** — Test morning (6-8 AM before workouts) vs. evening (7-9 PM post-workout)
5. **Keep mobile-first** — 75%+ of fitness app emails are opened on mobile
6. **Use progress visuals** — Charts, graphs, and streak visualizations outperform text-only emails
7. **Celebrate, don't nag** — Positive reinforcement outperforms guilt-based messaging by 2-3x

## 6. User-Generated Content (UGC)

### Why UGC Matters in Fitness

Fitness is inherently visual and personal. Transformation photos, workout logs, route maps, and achievement screenshots create authentic, high-converting content that no brand-produced material can match.

**Key Statistic:** UGC-based ads generate 4x higher click-through rates and 50% lower cost-per-acquisition than brand-produced ads across the fitness category.

### Strava: Stats Stickers and the Social Sharing Engine

**Strava Stats Stickers:**
- Transparent overlay "stickers" featuring distance, elevation gain, ride/run time, and a line drawing of the route map
- Users copy stickers to clipboard and overlay onto photos or import directly into Instagram Stories
- Creates branded Strava content that appears on millions of Instagram Stories weekly
- Spring 2025 updates expanded sticker customization options

**How Stats Stickers Drive Growth:**
1. User completes a run or ride on Strava
2. User creates a Stats Sticker with their activity data
3. User posts the sticker on their Instagram Story
4. Friends see the Strava-branded overlay and are prompted to join
5. New user downloads Strava, connects with the original user
6. Network effect compounds — more friends = more shared activities = more stickers

**Other Strava UGC Mechanisms:**
- Segments: community-created route leaderboards that drive competitive sharing
- Clubs: virtual running/cycling clubs where members share activities
- Challenges: monthly goals (e.g., "Run 100km this month") with achievement badges
- Year in Sport: annual summary infographic shared by millions each December
- Flyby: feature showing other Strava users on the same route at the same time

**Results:**
- 180M+ registered users, primarily through organic/word-of-mouth
- Users engage 35+ times per month
- 1.6M+ Instagram followers, 1.7M+ likes in 8 months
- Gen Z users plan to use Strava MORE while reducing Instagram/TikTok time

### Peloton: Community Content and the Instructor Economy

**UGC Mechanisms:**
- Leaderboard screenshots shared after challenging rides
- Milestone celebrations (100th ride, 500th ride) with on-screen shoutouts from instructors
- #PelotonMilestone and #OneMoreRide hashtags aggregate community content

**Transformation Content Best Practices:**
1. **Always require explicit consent** before using any transformation photo in marketing
2. **Include context** — timeframe, program followed, individual results may vary
3. **Diverse representation** — body types, ages, genders, abilities
4. **Avoid unrealistic expectations** — pair with disclaimers and realistic timelines
5. **Celebrate non-scale victories** — strength gains, consistency streaks, mental health improvements
